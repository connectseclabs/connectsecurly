What we're up against

The scams that reach your phone every week

They're convincing because they look ordinary. Here's what ConnectSecurly is trained to recognise — and what it tells you to do about it.

Fake KYC alerts

"Your account will be blocked today." A link that asks for Aadhaar, PAN or an OTP.

Courier & customs fraud

A parcel is "stuck". Pay a small fee, or verify your address on a lookalike site.

Fake police & "digital arrest"

Someone claiming to be an officer pressures you into transferring money to "clear" a case.

QR refund trick

You're told to scan a QR to receive money. Scanning a QR only ever sends it.

UPI collect requests

A payment request disguised as a refund, deposit or cashback. Approving it debits you.

Job & task scams

Easy work-from-home money — after a "registration fee" or a first small deposit.

FAQ

Questions people ask us

Is ConnectSecurly available to download?
Not yet — we're in early access. Email support@connectsecurly.com and we'll add you to the list and tell you when the app is ready.
What can I check with the scam checker?
WhatsApp messages, SMS, screenshots, links, QR codes, UPI IDs and phone numbers. The demo on this page accepts text only — the app handles screenshots and QR scanning.
Is the checker on this page the real thing?
It's a preview. It runs a simple pattern check entirely inside your browser so you can see the format of a result. It is not connected to our threat intelligence, and a "low risk" result here is not a guarantee that something is safe.
Does a "low risk" result mean it's definitely safe?
No. No scam checker catches everything, and new scams appear constantly. Treat the result as a second opinion. Where money or personal documents are involved, always verify through an official app or a phone number you already trust.
Can I use it to protect my parents?
That's what Family Safety Mode is for. You add trusted contacts, and warnings about risky messages or payments can reach both of you — so nobody has to make that decision alone.
I think I've already been scammed. What do I do?
Act fast. Call the national cybercrime helpline 1930 and file a report at cybercrime.gov.in, then contact your bank to freeze the account. Reporting within the first few hours gives you the best chance of recovering the money.
